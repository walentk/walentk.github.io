About Me

I'm a Research Associate with a strong background in Immuno-Oncology and Cancer Biology, currently based in New York City. My core work at MSKCC focuses on understanding the tumor microenvironment (TME) of Glioblastoma (GBM), with a particular interest in the role and functional heterogeneity of glioma-associated microglia and macrophages (GAMs).

My expertise spans both computational and wet-lab techniques, including novel sequencing methods (scRNA-seq), genetic animal models of GBM, flow cytometry, and bioinformatic analysis (R, Python). My ultimate goal is to leverage these insights to identify and validate new therapeutic targets and develop next-generation immunotherapies, such as CAR-macrophages, for aggressive brain tumors.
